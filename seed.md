# [Reality Seed]: The Idaho E-Verify Voter Mandate & Associated Economic Shock

## 1. Background Context (Memory Priming)
The State of Idaho is currently experiencing two conflicting macro-trends. Politically, it is a deeply conservative state with a Republican supermajority and high grassroots demand for election integrity and strict immigration enforcement. Economically, Idaho boasts a $10B+ agricultural industry (specifically dairy, potatoes, and sugar beets) and a booming residential construction sector. Both industries operate on tight profit margins and face severe labor shortages. It is an open secret within the state's business lobby that an estimated 40% to 50% of the agricultural and construction workforce consists of undocumented immigrants or mixed-status families. Historically, there has been an unspoken detente between strict political rhetoric and the realities of these labor needs. 

## 2. The Catalyst (The Injectable Variable)
The Idaho State Legislature passes, and the Governor immediately signs, the "Secure Idaho Elections Act" (SIEA). The law mandates that county clerks must run every currently registered voter, and all new registrants, through the federal E-Verify database to confirm citizenship before the upcoming November election. 

Crucially, Section 4 of the SIEA includes a "Database Integration Clause." This mandates that the state's voter registration system, DMV records, and state tax records share data. Any individual residing in the same household as a flagged non-citizen, or anyone who attempts to register and fails the E-Verify check, will have their data automatically forwarded to the State Department of Labor and state police for investigation of voter fraud and illegal employment.

## 3. Vector of Delivery
The mandate is injected into the simulation via a live-streamed press conference from the Idaho State Capitol at 10:00 AM MDT on a Tuesday. The Governor’s office simultaneously posts a threaded announcement on X (Twitter), claiming this will "make Idaho elections the most secure in the nation." Ten minutes later, a major immigrant advocacy group posts a viral breakdown of Section 4 on TikTok, and translated warnings begin spreading uncontrollably through Spanish-language WhatsApp groups used by agricultural workers in rural counties (e.g., Canyon, Twin Falls, Jerome).

## 4. Key Entities & Factions
- **Faction A: The Idaho Agricultural & Construction Lobby (Corporate & Private)** 
  - *Motivation:* Pure economic survival. They need their workforce to stay in the state to harvest crops, milk cows, and frame houses. They tend to vote conservative but prioritize business continuity.
- **Faction B: Migrant and Mixed-Status Laborers** 
  - *Motivation:* Safety, family unity, and income. They form the backbone of the local supply chain. Their primary driver is avoiding exposure to law enforcement. They are highly mobile.
- **Faction C: Grassroots Election Security Voters** 
  - *Motivation:* Ensuring zero non-citizen voting. Highly active on X and local Facebook groups. They view any economic argument against the bill as an admission of employing illegal labor.
- **Faction D: County Clerks and State Bureaucrats**
  - *Motivation:* System stability. They are tasked with implementing an employment database (E-Verify) for electoral purposes, dealing with the historical 1.5% to 2% error rate (which often flags naturalized citizens or women with recent name changes).
- **Constraints:** The law takes effect in late August, exactly 30 days before the critical fall potato and sugar beet harvest season begins. Cows on mega-dairies must be milked 2 to 3 times a day without interruption. Neighboring states (Washington and Oregon) currently have open agricultural jobs and do not have this mandate.

## 5. ReportAgent Directive
Observe and report exclusively on the emergent macroeconomic and supply-chain outcomes over a 60-day simulated period. Track the following metrics:
1. **Labor Flight Velocity:** Track the simulated out-migration rate of Faction B to Washington/Oregon. At what timestamp do mega-dairies and construction projects begin to halt operations?
2. **Lobbying Friction:** Log all simulated communications and political fractures between Faction A (Ag-Biz) and state politicians. Do business owners publicly turn against the Governor, or quietly attempt to fund legal injunctions to block Section 4? 
3. **Localized Inflation & Spoilage:** Calculate the simulated localized inflation on food and housing, and the estimated tonnage of crop spoilage if the labor supply drops by 10%, 20%, or 30%.
4. **Local Retail Collapse:** Monitor secondary economic impacts—track consumer spending at local grocers, auto shops, and retail in heavily agricultural counties as the migrant workforce stops spending cash locally or hoards it for relocation.
